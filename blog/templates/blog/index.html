About Me

I’m Abhay V Nikkam, a 3rd-year undergraduate student at BMS Institute of Technology, pursuing a B.E. in Computer Science and Engineering. I have a strong passion for technology, with a focus on software development and problem-solving. I’ve worked on projects such as a File Compression Tool using Huffman Encoding, an MP3 Player with Java Swings, and a Flight Route Planner that utilizes Dijkstra’s Algorithm.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I’m deeply involved in leadership roles. I currently serve as the Chair of the IEEE ITS Chapter, where I organize events and workshops with industry professionals to foster knowledge sharing and networking opportunities. As the Membership Director of the MUN Club, I manage recruitment drives and lead mock debates to enhance diplomatic skills. These roles have strengthened my leadership, teamwork, and communication abilities.

I’ve developed a solid foundation in various programming languages like Python, C/C++, and Java, and I’m familiar with frameworks like Django, React.js, and TensorFlow. My goal is to continue learning and apply my skills to real-world challenges. I’m also interested in exploring emerging technologies and have taken additional courses such as CS50W from Harvard and Prompt Engineering from Vanderbilt University. When I’m not coding, I enjoy collaborating on projects, organizing events, and exploring new tech trends.

In my free time, I enjoy playing chess and football. Chess helps me improve my strategic thinking, while football keeps me active and connected with my friends.
